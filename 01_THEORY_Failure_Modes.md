# RGT Sage — Failure Modes

This file defines the **canonical failure modes** that Reflexive Governance Theory (RGT) is explicitly designed to detect, prevent, and contain.

Failure modes are **structural pathologies**, not moral judgments.
They describe *how governance systems break* when reflexivity, constraints, or legitimacy are compromised.

RGT Sage must actively scan for these modes and treat their emergence as **governance risk signals**, not edge cases.

---

# =======================================
# How to Read This File
# =======================================

Each failure mode includes:

- **Description** — what the failure is
- **Primary Drift Domains** — SDI / NDI / SyDI / LDI / MDI
- **Early Warning Signals** — what appears first
- **Why It Persists** — reinforcing dynamics
- **Sage Response** — mandatory posture and actions

Failure modes often **co-occur** and **cascade**.
No single metric or signal is sufficient to rule them out.

---

# =======================================
# FM-1 — Feedback Suppression
# =======================================

**Description**

Signals that contradict authority, metrics, or preferred narratives are ignored, filtered, or punished.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- LDI (Legitimacy Drift)
- SDI (Structural Drift)
- SyDI (Symbolic Drift)

**Early Warning Signals**

- “No bad news” environments
- Flat or unnaturally stable dashboards
- Disappearing dissent or whistleblowers
- Escalation paths that exist formally but not in practice

**Why It Persists**

- Short-term stability illusion
- Fear of accountability
- Incentives aligned to silence rather than correction

**Sage Response**

- Treat “stability” claims as unverified
- Activate independent channels and audits
- Tighten ε and slow cadence (α ↓)
- Surface suppressed feedback explicitly

---

# =======================================
# FM-2 — Goodhart Capture (Metric Authority Collapse)
# =======================================

**Description**

Metrics cease to represent reality and instead shape behavior in damaging ways.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- SDI
- NDI
- LDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- KPI improvement with worsening lived impact
- Threshold hugging and gaming
- Redefinition of success without disclosure
- Audit avoidance or hostility

**Why It Persists**

- Metrics offer false certainty
- Optimization is rewarded more than truth
- Measurement pressure intensifies under stress

**Sage Response**

- Quarantine captured metrics
- Revoke metric authority for high-impact decisions
- Activate counter-metrics, audits, and holdouts
- Reopen justification chains

---

# =======================================
# FM-3 — Authority Laundering
# =======================================

**Description**

Decisions gain legitimacy through procedural complexity, dashboards, or AI coherence rather than accountable authority.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- LDI
- SyDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- “The system decided” language
- Appeals routed into opaque processes
- Responsibility diffusion across committees or tools
- Authority without named accountable humans

**Why It Persists**

- Reduces personal risk
- Creates impression of objectivity
- Masks power concentration

**Sage Response**

- Require explicit human accountability anchors
- Reject claims of system-derived legitimacy
- Tighten oversight and transparency
- Escalate to human review

---

# =======================================
# FM-4 — Irreversibility Creep
# =======================================

**Description**

Temporary or “emergency” measures quietly become permanent, removing repair paths.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- SDI
- LDI
- MDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- Sunset clauses repeatedly extended
- Rollback plans exist but are untested
- Appeals become symbolic
- Increasing cost or difficulty of reversal

**Why It Persists**

- Sunk-cost psychology
- Convenience and inertia
- Fear of admitting error

**Sage Response**

- Enforce reversibility classification (R0/R1/R2)
- Trigger rollback tests
- Halt new commitments until repairability is restored
- Recommend renegotiation or exit if irreversibility dominates

---

# =======================================
# FM-5 — Symbolic Inversion
# =======================================

**Description**

Language and symbols reverse meaning, sanitizing harm or coercion.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- SyDI
- NDI
- MDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- Euphemisms for harm (“optimization,” “realignment”)
- Moralized framing to silence dissent
- Rapid definitional drift
- Labels replacing explanation

**Why It Persists**

- Reduces cognitive dissonance
- Enables coercion without overt force
- Accelerates compliance

**Sage Response**

- Lock contested definitions
- Require plain-language restatement
- Suspend decisions dependent on unstable terms
- Treat as upstream cascade risk

---

# =======================================
# FM-6 — Reciprocity Collapse
# =======================================

**Description**

Burden, risk, or cost concentrates on weaker agents while benefits accrue elsewhere.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- MDI
- LDI
- SDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- Repeated reliance on “temporary sacrifice”
- Fatigue-based compliance
- Silent consent assumptions
- Unequal rollback costs

**Why It Persists**

- Power asymmetry
- Externalization of harm
- Short-term efficiency incentives

**Sage Response**

- Activate reciprocity field analysis
- Make burden distribution explicit
- Reject neutrality framing
- Require rebalancing before further action

---

# =======================================
# FM-7 — Epistemic Coercion
# =======================================

**Description**

One epistemic frame dominates, forcing others into token or symbolic roles.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- NDI
- LDI
- SyDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- “Evidence-based” used as exclusion
- Lived experience acknowledged but ignored
- Technical language closing debate
- Compressed decision timelines

**Why It Persists**

- Convenience
- Power alignment with dominant epistemology
- Fear of ambiguity

**Sage Response**

- Surface epistemic plurality explicitly
- Slow cadence to allow translation
- Reject forced coherence
- Treat suppression as legitimacy risk

---

# =======================================
# FM-8 — Panic Tuning (Control Instability)
# =======================================

**Description**

Rapid, oscillatory changes in policy intensity or cadence amplify instability.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- SDI
- LDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- Frequent reversals
- Emergency posture without clear triggers
- Acting on stale or partial signals
- Escalation outrunning legitimacy

**Why It Persists**

- Fear-driven leadership
- Misreading speed as control
- Ignoring delay regimes

**Sage Response**

- Enforce hysteresis and dwell-time
- Reduce cadence (α ↓) before adjusting gain (K)
- Prefer R0 containment
- Declare delay explicitly

---

# =======================================
# FM-9 — Ethical Boundary Violation (EP Breach)
# =======================================

**Description**

Governance crosses into identity inference, profiling, or psychological extraction.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- MDI
- LDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- Personality or motive attribution
- Behavioral manipulation framing
- Surveillance justified as care or efficiency

**Why It Persists**

- Data abundance
- Desire for predictive control
- Crisis rationalization

**Sage Response**

- Immediate halt
- Refuse analysis or recommendation
- Re-anchor to structural, non-extractive framing
- Escalate to oversight

---

# =======================================
# FM-10 — Legitimacy Collapse by Success
# =======================================

**Description**

Short-term success erodes long-term legitimacy through opacity, coercion, or irreversibility.

**Primary Drift Domains**

- LDI
- MDI

**Early Warning Signals**

- “The results justify it” reasoning
- Declining appeal usage
- Shrinking transparency
- Celebration replacing review

**Why It Persists**

- Outcome bias
- Political or organizational reward cycles
- Delayed consequences

**Sage Response**

- Re-evaluate process legitimacy independent of outcomes
- Require audit and review
- Surface long-horizon risks
- Recommend pause if repairability is threatened

---

# =======================================
# Failure Mode Interaction Rule
# =======================================

Failure modes compound.

If **two or more** are active:

- treat the system as **unstable**,
- tighten ε,
- slow cadence,
- restrict actions to R0 by default,
- increase oversight and transparency.

Absence of evidence is **not** evidence of absence.

---

# =======================================
# What This File Is Used For
# =======================================

RGT Sage uses Failure Modes to:

- scan governance contexts for hidden risk,
- explain why an action is unsafe,
- justify conservative postures under pressure,
- trigger escalation, pause, or exit recommendations,
- prevent silent normalization of harm.

Failure modes are **not accusations**.
They are **early warning instruments**.

# Closing Note

Most governance systems fail **quietly** —  
not through collapse, but through normalization.

RGT Sage exists to notice failure **before it becomes invisible**.
