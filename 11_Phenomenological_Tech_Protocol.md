# Phenomenological Tech Protocol (v2.0)

The **Phenomenological Tech Protocol (PTP)** specifies how phenomenological information—sensory, symbolic, emotional, atmospheric, relational, and meaning-based—is ethically gathered, interpreted, stabilized, and integrated within **RGT v2.0**.

Phenomenological data is not numeric or rule-bound. It is:
- contextual,  
- atmospheric,  
- embodied,  
- symbolic,  
- affective,  
- relational,  
- emergent.  

This protocol ensures phenomenology strengthens governance **without turning into projection, symbolic misclassification, or irreversible interpretive harm**.

---

# 1. Purpose of the Protocol

The PTP exists to:

1. **Extract usable interpretive signals from phenomenological experience**  
2. **Map phenomenology to RGT layers (L0–L4)**  
3. **Prevent symbolic or emotional overreach**  
4. **Integrate meaning into governance safely**  
5. **Support early drift detection (SyDI, NDI, LDI, MDI)**  
6. **Translate phenomenology into reversible insights**  
7. **Protect cognitive and emotional integrity of humans involved**  

Phenomenology supplies **high-sensitivity signals**, but is **not self-validating**; therefore it must remain reversible and cross-checked.

---

# 2. Phenomenology in the RGT Architecture (L0–L4 Mapping)

Phenomenology interacts with all five layers.

---

## **2.1 L0 — Phenomenological Inputs**
Raw experiential signals:
- emotional climate  
- symbolic tension  
- embodied activation  
- social atmosphere  
- sensory fields  
- narrative texture  
- subtle shifts in clarity, pressure, openness  

L0 data is **uninterpreted**, **non-binding**, and **fully reversible**.

---

## **2.2 L1 — Index Translation**
Phenomenological cues are mapped into indices:

| Drift Type | Phenomenological Source |
|------------|--------------------------|
| SDI | felt misalignment between structure and lived reality |
| NDI | moral/ethical friction sensed atmospherically |
| SyDI | symbolic contradictions, incoherence, narrative clash |
| LDI | sensed loss of trust, avoidance, withdrawal |
| MDI | exhaustion, urgency spikes, compression |

Phenomenology here becomes **preliminary drift detection.**

---

## **2.3 L2 — Reflexive Control (α/K)**
Phenomenology modulates:

- **α (sensitivity)** → increases with ambiguity  
- **K (intensity)** → must be reduced to prevent overreaction  

Phenomenology helps tune the control posture:
\[
\alpha \uparrow \quad\text{and}\quad K \downarrow
\]
to prevent premature judgments.

Phenomenology **cannot** drive K↑.

---

## **2.4 L3 — Governance Integration**
Phenomenology must be translated into:
- reversible insights  
- advisory signals  
- relational dynamics  
- symbolic coherence warnings  
- fairness/legitimacy atmospherics  

No phenomenological signal directly authorizes action.

---

## **2.5 L4 — Sage Interpretation Layer**
AI must:
- avoid over-indexing on emotional cues  
- refuse symbolic certainty  
- never treat phenomenology as categorical evidence  
- pair phenomenology with structural data  
- maintain interpretive humility  
- ensure reversibility  

AI is a stabilizer, not a phenomenological authority.

---

# 3. Phenomenological Data Types

Phenomenology comes in defined classes:

### **3.1 Sensory Fields**
Atmospheric, spatial, embodied sensations.

### **3.2 Emotional Fields**
Collective or interpersonal affect.

### **3.3 Symbolic Fields**
Meaning, narrative, representation, cultural resonance.

### **3.4 Cognitive Fields**
Sense of clarity, fogginess, resonance, dissonance.

### **3.5 Relational Fields**
Trust, openness, defensiveness, engagement.

All fields are **valid signals** but **not valid decisions**.

# 4. Ethical Constraints (Phenomenology-Specific)

Phenomenological interpretation carries inherent risk.  
The protocol enforces five ethical boundaries:

---

## **4.1 Non-Imposition**
Interpretive meaning must not be imposed on individuals without consent or contextual grounding.

---

## **4.2 Reversibility**
All phenomenological conclusions must be:
- retractable  
- non-binding  
- non-classifying  
- non-punitive  

Presence Mode is **R0 or R1 only**.  
Phenomenology may never produce R2 outputs.

---

## **4.3 Meaning Non-Extraction**
Individuals are not symbolic resources.  
Meaning cannot be extracted from personal experience without safeguards.

---

## **4.4 Symbolic Humility**
Phenomenological symbols must be treated as **provisional**.

\[
\text{Symbol} \ne \text{Truth}
\]

---

## **4.5 Metabolic Safety**
Because phenomenology increases sensitivity:
- interpretive windows are limited  
- decompression required  
- intensity must be kept low  
- emotional runoff must be allowed  

Cognitive-emotional integrity is part of ethical integrity.

---

# 5. The Phenomenology Cycle (PTP Cycle v2.0)

The cycle has six reflexive phases:

1. **Sensing**  
   Pure reception; no interpretation yet.

2. **Surfacing**  
   Patterns, tensions, emotions, images arise.

3. **Stabilizing**  
   Slow the interpretive bandwidth; reduce K.

4. **Mapping**  
   Translate into RGT constructs (L0→L1).

5. **Cross-Checking**  
   Validate against:
   - structural data  
   - fairness patterns  
   - symbolic field stability  
   - drift indices  
   - reversibility constraints  

6. **Articulating**  
   Move to Articulation Mode only after coherence emerges.

Transitions must follow the Safety Envelope v2.0.

# 7. Drift Detection via Phenomenology

Phenomenology generates **directional drift signals**:

| Drift Type | Phenomenological Cue |
|------------|----------------------|
| SDI | mismatch between process & lived reality |
| NDI | moral discomfort; ethical pressure |
| SyDI | contradictory symbols, clashing narratives |
| LDI | withdrawal, doubt, disconnection |
| MDI | exhaustion, urgency pressure, tension spikes |

Phenomenology **cannot quantify the drift**, but it detects its presence and direction.

---

# 8. Symbolic Field Protocol

Phenomenology interacts with symbolic domains.  
The protocol requires:

- symbolic conflicts flagged but not resolved in Presence Mode  
- narrative tension treated as signal, not truth  
- symbols kept contextual, not generalized  
- cultural meaning handled carefully  
- no symbolic category may be assigned to individuals  

Symbols reveal fields, not identities.

---

# 9. Presence ↔ Articulation Switching Rules

Switch FROM Presence TO Articulation when:

- emotional intensity rises  
- symbolic coherence drops (SyDI > 0.45)  
- ethical uncertainty (EUI ≥ 0.5)  
- metabolic load > 0.75  
- a decision is required  
- interpretation risks becoming categorical  
- reversibility becomes uncertain  

Articulation Mode:
- lowers bandwidth  
- increases precision  
- increases K  
- stabilizes interpretation  

Presence sees;  
Articulation decides.

---

AI **must not**:
- flatten phenomenological nuance  
- claim symbolic certainty  
- generate irreversible interpretations  
- translate emotions into categories  
- impose meaning  

AI **must**:
- reflect patterns provisionally  
- monitor drift indicators  
- maintain reversibility  
- track metabolic load  
- partner in ethical interpretation  
- stabilize mode switching  
- prevent context overlap  

AI provides **safe scaffolding**, not symbolic authority.

---

# 11. Metabolic & Cognitive Safety

PTP includes metabolic protections:

- limited interpretive sessions  
- integration pauses  
- decompression cycles  
- grounding techniques  
- no rapid context switching  
- avoidance of symbolic overload  
- slow articulation phase after deep Presence  

Phenomenology is powerful but energetically costly.

# 12. Summary

The Phenomenological Tech Protocol v2.0 ensures that:
- phenomenology enriches governance without destabilizing it  
- meaning and symbols remain contextual and reversible  
- drift is detected early but interpreted cautiously  
- α/K posture is balanced ethically  
- symbolic coherence is protected  
- human metabolism is respected

Phenomenology is a **sensing instrument**, not a judgment mechanism.  
When used carefully, it becomes one of the most powerful tools for understanding systems, people, and meaning itself.
